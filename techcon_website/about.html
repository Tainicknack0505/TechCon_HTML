History of TechCon

TechCon® was first held in 1996 in New Orleans, Louisiana. It has since moved about the U.S., accommodating the growing number of delegates and utility hosts. It often includes a regional facility tour of the sponsoring utility. Most recently, the conference averaged 200 in attendance representing 11 various countries and 30 interactive exhibiting corporations. TechCon offers a vibrant and unparalleled learning experience with top-notch topics and speakers.

Mission of TechCon

TechCon® Training and Expo has a trusted history of providing industry experts to lead discussions on the technical development and solutions which improve key high voltage maintenance programs and asset management strategies for the aging electrical grid infrastructure. Since 1996, TechCon has been produced by TJ|H2b Analytical Services, a senior leader in the oil analysis industry. TechCon® 2024 is hosted by Pacific Gas and Electric PG&E.

Notable Past Speakers at TechCon


Abhijit Mahindroo: Abhijit Mahindroo is a Senior Partner at McKinsey & Company, specializing in the semiconductor, industrial, and automotive sectors. He co-leads the Semiconductors practice in the Americas, advising clients on strategy and operations issues. Some of his recent work includes: Developing integration-support programs for industry-defining mergers across the semiconductor value chain. Creating operations-transformation programs that improved throughput, cost, and cycle time for semiconductor and industrial companies. Leading R&D-optimization initiatives to enhance effectiveness and efficiency. Generating growth-strategy programs for core and noncore businesses in the tech and automotive sectors. Abhijit also co-led the acquisition and integration of Numetrics, an R&D planning and benchmarking solution.


Meredith Perry: Meredith Perry is an American inventor, entrepreneur, and scientist. She gained fame for inventing uBeam, a wireless charging system based on ultrasound. Meredith studied paleobiology and astrobiology at the University of Pennsylvania, conducting research with NASA. She co-authored astrobiology research papers and won the university’s Invention Competition for uBeam. After her uBeam journey, Meredith founded Elemind Technologies, focusing on noninvasive neurotechnology. Elemind recently emerged from stealth mode, with a wearable device that can address essential tremors, induce sleep, and enhance memory formation. Meredith’s accolades include being recognized by Fast Company, Fortune, Forbes, and Vanity Fair.


Mitch Thrower: Mitch Thrower wears multiple hats: financier, entrepreneur, author, and 22X Ironman triathlete. He founded Events.com, a global SaaS platform that empowers event organizers to create, promote, and enhance events. Mitch also serves as Chairman of The La Jolla Foundation, which focuses on Project Active—sending sports equipment to children in war zones. Previously, he co-founded Active.com (The Active Network, Inc.), which went public before being acquired for $1.05 billion by Vista. Mitch’s journey spans entrepreneurship, media, and philanthropy, making him a dynamic figure in the tech industry.
